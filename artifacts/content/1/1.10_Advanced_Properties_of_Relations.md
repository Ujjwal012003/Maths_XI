## 1.10 Improper Integrals and Their Convergence

![Figure 1.10: Graphical representation of an improper integral on an infinite interval](images/fig1.10.png)

Improper integrals arise when we integrate a function over an unbounded interval or when the integrand has an infinite discontinuity within the interval of integration. This section introduces improper integrals, discusses their convergence, and illustrates methods to evaluate them.

### 1.10.1 Improper Integrals over Infinite Intervals

![Figure 1.10.1: Area under the curve of \( f(x) = \frac{1}{x^2} \) over \([1, \infty)\)](images/fig1.10.1.png)

Consider the integral of a function \( f(x) \) over an infinite interval \([a, \infty)\):

\[
\int_a^\infty f(x) \, dx.
\]

Because the upper limit is infinite, we define this improper integral as a limit:

\[
\int_a^\infty f(x) \, dx = \lim_{t \to \infty} \int_a^t f(x) \, dx.
\]

If this limit exists (is finite), the improper integral is said to converge; otherwise, it diverges.

**Example 1.10.1**

Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x^2} \, dx\) and determine if it converges.

**Solution:**

1. Compute the definite integral with a finite upper limit \(t > 1\):

\[
\int_1^t \frac{1}{x^2} \, dx = \int_1^t x^{-2} \, dx.
\]

2. Find the antiderivative:

\[
\int x^{-2} \, dx = -x^{-1} + C = -\frac{1}{x} + C.
\]

3. Evaluate from 1 to \(t\):

\[
\int_1^t \frac{1}{x^2} \, dx = \left[-\frac{1}{x}\right]_1^t = -\frac{1}{t} + 1 = 1 - \frac{1}{t}.
\]

4. Take the limit as \( t \to \infty \):

\[
\lim_{t \to \infty} \left(1 - \frac{1}{t}\right) = 1 - 0 = 1.
\]

Since the limit exists and is finite, the integral converges and

\[
\int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x^2} \, dx = 1.
\]

### 1.10.2 Improper Integrals with Infinite Discontinuities

![Figure 1.10.2: Function \( f(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \) with an infinite discontinuity at \( x = 0 \)](images/fig1.10.2.png)

An improper integral may also arise if the integrand becomes unbounded at a point within the interval of integration. Suppose \( f(x) \) is continuous on \([a,b)\) but unbounded as \( x \to b^- \). Then the improper integral is defined as

\[
\int_a^b f(x) \, dx = \lim_{t \to b^-} \int_a^t f(x) \, dx,
\]

provided this limit exists.

**Example 1.10.2**

Determine whether the integral \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx\) converges.

**Solution:**

1. Because the integrand is unbounded at \( x=0 \), write:

\[
\int_0^1 \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx = \lim_{\epsilon \to 0^+} \int_\epsilon^1 x^{-\frac{1}{2}} \, dx.
\]

2. Find the antiderivative:

\[
\int x^{-\frac{1}{2}} \, dx = 2x^{\frac{1}{2}} + C = 2\sqrt{x} + C.
\]

3. Evaluate the integral:

\[
\int_\epsilon^1 \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}}\, dx = \left[2\sqrt{x}\right]_\epsilon^1 = 2 - 2\sqrt{\epsilon}.
\]

4. Take the limit as \( \epsilon \to 0^+ \):

\[
\lim_{\epsilon \to 0^+} (2 - 2\sqrt{\epsilon}) = 2 - 0 = 2.
\]

Since the limit is finite, the improper integral converges and

\[
\int_0^1 \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx = 2.
\]

### 1.10.3 Tests for Convergence of Improper Integrals

![Figure 1.10.3: Illustration of comparison test for improper integrals](images/fig1.10.3.png)

To determine whether improper integrals converge, we often use comparison tests. Suppose \( f(x) \) and \( g(x) \) are functions such that \( 0 \leq f(x) \leq g(x) \) for all \( x \) in \([a, \infty)\).

- If \(\displaystyle \int_a^\infty g(x) \, dx\) converges, then so does \(\displaystyle \int_a^\infty f(x) \, dx\).
- If \(\displaystyle \int_a^\infty f(x) \, dx\) diverges, then so does \(\displaystyle \int_a^\infty g(x) \, dx\).

**Example 1.10.3**

Does the integral \(\displaystyle \int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x \sqrt{x^2 + 1}} \, dx\) converge?

**Solution:**

1. We observe that for \( x \geq 1 \),

\[
\sqrt{x^2 + 1} \geq \sqrt{x^2} = x.
\]

Thus,

\[
\frac{1}{x \sqrt{x^2 + 1}} \leq \frac{1}{x \cdot x} = \frac{1}{x^2}.
\]

2. Since \(\int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x^2} \, dx\) is convergent (see Example 1.10.1), by the comparison test, the integral

\[
\int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x \sqrt{x^2 + 1}} \, dx
\]

also converges.

3. For completeness, we can compute the integral explicitly:

\[
\int \frac{1}{x \sqrt{x^2 + 1}} \, dx.
\]

Use the substitution \( t = \sqrt{x^2 + 1} \), then

\[
t^2 = x^2 + 1 \implies 2t \, dt = 2x \, dx \implies t \, dt = x \, dx.
\]

Rewrite the integral:

\[
\int \frac{1}{x \cdot t} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{x \cdot t} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{x \cdot t} \, dx.
\]

Substituting \( x \, dx = t \, dt \), we get

\[
\int \frac{1}{x t} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{t} \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{t} \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{t} \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx,
\]

which requires care. Alternatively, proceed differently:

Rewrite the integrand as

\[
\frac{1}{x \sqrt{x^2+1}} = \frac{1}{x \sqrt{x^2+1}}.
\]

Set \( x = \sinh u \), so

\[
dx = \cosh u \, du, \quad \sqrt{x^2+1} = \sqrt{\sinh^2 u + 1} = \cosh u.
\]

The integrand becomes

\[
\frac{1}{\sinh u \cdot \cosh u} \cdot dx = \frac{1}{\sinh u \cdot \cosh u} \cdot \cosh u \, du = \frac{1}{\sinh u} \, du.
\]

So,

\[
\int \frac{1}{x \sqrt{x^2+1}} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{\sinh u} \, du.
\]

Since \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{\sinh u} = \csch u\),

\[
\int \csch u \, du = \ln \left|\tanh \frac{u}{2}\right| + C.
\]

Returning to \( x \):

\[
u = \sinh^{-1} x,
\]

thus

\[
\int \frac{1}{x \sqrt{x^2 +1}} \, dx = \ln \left| \tanh \frac{ \sinh^{-1} x }{2} \right| + C.
\]

4. Evaluating the improper integral from 1 to \(\infty\):

\[
\int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x \sqrt{x^2 + 1}} \, dx = \lim_{t \to \infty} \left[ \ln \left| \tanh \frac{ \sinh^{-1} x }{2} \right| \right]_1^t = \lim_{t \to \infty} \ln \left| \tanh \frac{ \sinh^{-1} t }{2} \right| - \ln \left| \tanh \frac{ \sinh^{-1} 1 }{2} \right|.
\]

5. As \( t \to \infty \), \( \sinh^{-1} t \to \infty \), so

\[
\tanh \frac{ \sinh^{-1} t }{2} \to 1.
\]

Therefore,

\[
\lim_{t \to \infty} \ln \left| \tanh \frac{ \sinh^{-1} t }{2} \right| = \ln 1 = 0,
\]

and thus the integral converges to

\[
- \ln \left| \tanh \frac{ \sinh^{-1} 1 }{2} \right|.
\]

Since \(\ln {\big| \tanh ( \sinh^{-1} 1 /2 ) \big|} > 0\), the integral evaluates to a positive finite number.

---

This completes the discussion on improper integrals and their convergence.
